To tell a story well

Books are judged by their covers. Products and apps are judged by their UI. But, that's only part of the story. To tell a story or to shape an experience that keeps people reading and interacting, the design must begin well before photos or fonts are selected. I practice design informed and re-formed by knowing real users. Here is a sampling of my visual design work.

Working as the sole designer for a bi-monthly magazine for moms, I honed my ability to tell stories in unique ways (a flowchart showing one mom's failures and successes at eating healthy) and to create and curate strong visuals.

For the same client, I worked as part of a team to rebuild the entire structure of the magazine and to redefine the tone and style, sourcing photography from readers whenever possible.
