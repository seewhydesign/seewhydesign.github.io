During my years as a ux / web designer, I continously worked to care for and curate the experience for the diverse groups of people visiting the site.

The site needed to support the needs for both the technical medical professionals and their patients and family members. We also needed to consider the media, the general public, and policy.

Back to basics: rethinking the homepage

Before the redesign

Feeling lost

Site visitors reported problems locating what they hoped to find.

Conducting user interviews and usability sessions, we discovered multiple issues to address.

A lengthy homepage packed with technical, scientific news articles, while needed by transplant professionals, confused or frustrated the many patients and general public visitors.

The navigation, which was heavily used by site visitors, didn't have groupings that made sense to all audiences.

After the redesign

Welcoming and helpful

The navigation now reflects the key audience segments and offers direct links to the content they most frequently visit.

Key messages about the non-profit's purpose and aims were featured in the hero area to help grow understanding across audiences.

Based on both analytics and usability sessions, I shortened the homepage length and helped develop guidance on

Those needed technical, scientific news items were housed in a redesigned news section specifically for donation and transplant professionals.
